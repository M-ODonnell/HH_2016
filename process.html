    Step 1: Decision

    Prepare to wait. The minimum length of the transition process is 2 years and it may extend for 3, 4, 5 or even more years. This is a dangerous and irreversible process and should only be considered if the only other option for you is transition or die.

  • Step 2: Find a Doctor

    Any physician that can prescribe hormones will work - but doctors have prejudices too so be sure to do your research and find someone who is willing to work with you.

  • Step 3: Begin Hormone Treatment

    Contact your doctor for a customized list of hormones and their appropriate doses.

  • Step 4: Monitor Changes

    Watch for complications and treat your body as you would treat a newborn child. Your body is literally re-developing so adding chemicals such as tobacco, alcohol or other drugs may have permanent adverse effects.   • Step 5: Gender Expression

    Your social gender performance will be a test which you must pass in order to be accepted as the gender you identify. Your gender expression pertains to the small nuances of male or female behaviour which will allow others to see you as the person that you are. This is where a gender therapist, speech therapist, or other specialized professional may be a huge asset. View our list of therapists to connect with local therapists.
